AquaS I

The water quality measurement drone is a UAV designed to gather precise data on the quality of freshwater bodies. Equipped with specialized sensors, remote sampling capabilities, and advanced navigation technology, this drone measures parameters such as temperature, pH, dissolved oxygen, turbidity, and contaminants. With real-time data transmission, it enables efficient monitoring and analysis, making it a valuable tool for assessing water quality in freshwater bodies.

Objetive

The main objective of the water quality measurement drone is to gather accurate data on the water quality in freshwater bodies such as rivers and lakes by measuring key parameters.

Drone features

The water quality measurement drone allows for easy and multiple samplings. With an intuitive functionality, users can program flight routes and specific sampling points in bodies of water. The approximate flight time varies, but it is typically around 30 minutes to 1 hour, providing enough time to collect relevant data in different areas.

Adaptability

The drone system is highly adaptable and can be mounted on other unmanned aerial platforms. This allows harnessing the capabilities and functionalities of the water quality measurement system on different drones. By being compatible with various platforms, it facilitates data collection of water quality in diverse environments and expands monitoring capacity in a flexible and versatile manner.

AquaScan I

The AquaS I model for water quality measurement aims to conserve and protect water resources to help the planet. Working together, it collects precise data to make informed decisions in the management of aquatic ecosystems. Its goal is to address pollution, promote sustainable practices, and preserve water quality and marine life. The team is committed to minimizing environmental impact and maximizing efficiency by utilizing advanced technology and adapting to different drones. Their mission is to achieve significant results in the long-term conservation and protection of water resources.
